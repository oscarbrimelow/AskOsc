Frequently Asked Questions

What is a Transaction Coordinator?
A Transaction Coordinator (TC) is a real estate professional who manages all the administrative tasks and paperwork involved in a transaction from the moment it goes under contract until it closes. We handle deadlines, documentation, and communication to ensure a smooth process.
Why do I need one?
By delegating the administrative work to a TC, a real estate agent saves valuable time—up to 16 hours per transaction. This allows you to focus on high-value activities like lead generation, showing properties, and negotiating deals, ultimately helping you grow your business.
Are you licensed?
I do not hold a real estate license. A specific Transaction Coordinator license is not required in the state of Ohio. My expertise comes from direct, hands-on experience and a deep understanding of the residential closing process, and I operate with the highest level of professionalism and care.
Do you work with commercial or land deals?
No. To provide the most focused and expert service possible, I specialize exclusively in residential real estate transactions. I do not handle commercial or land-only deals.
